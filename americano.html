Americano Coffee Selection

Classic Americano

Tasting Notes: Smooth and balanced—bold espresso aroma with lightly diluted intensity, retaining crema complexity yet more mellow than straight espresso

Recommended Brew: Pull one or two shots of espresso (approx. 45–50 ml total)
Add 90–100 ml of hot water (≈93–95 °C) into a preheated cup
Pour espresso over the water (preserves crema), or water first based on preference

$4

Long Black Americano

Tasting Notes: Stronger espresso-forward flavor with pronounced crema and slightly less dilution than an Americano

Recommended Brew: Add about 100–120 ml hot water first.
Pour a double espresso (≈60 ml) gently on top to preserve crema

$4.5

Short Americano

Tasting Notes: Very bold and concentrated—nearly espresso strength with minimal dilution

Recommended Brew: Pull one shot of espresso.
Add an equal amount of hot water (1:1 ratio)
Result is richer and more intense than standard Americanos

$5

Americano Misto

Tasting Notes: Result is richer and more intense than standard Americanos

Recommended Brew: Make a classic Americano (espresso + hot water).
Add a splash of steamed milk or light foam (~50% water, ~25% espresso, ~25% milk).

$3.9

Iced Americano

Tasting Notes: Crisp and refreshing, clean coffee flavor with cool finish—not creamy, but light and energizing

Recommended Brew: Pull one or two espresso shots.
Fill glass with ice.
Pour cold water or cooled espresso over ice.
Add espresso last or first, depending on preference

$4.9

Red Eye

Tasting Notes: Very strong and high‑caffeine—robust espresso flavor bolstered by drip coffee base, smooth yet punchy.

Recommended Brew: Brew a cup of drip coffee.
Pour one shot of espresso into it.
Stir lightly—intense in both body and caffeine content

$4
